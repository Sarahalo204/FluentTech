### Error 1: Article Omission with Countable Nouns
**Linguistic cause:** Arabic often omits articles in contexts where English requires "a" or "the." 
**❌ Example:** I need report by tomorrow.
**✅ Correction:** I need a report by tomorrow.
**Frequency:** High  **CEFR level where fixed:** A1

### Error 2: Overuse of Definite Article
**Linguistic cause:** Arabic uses the definite article more generally, leading to extra "the" in English. 
**❌ Example:** The server is down. I check the logs.
**✅ Correction:** The server is down. I checked the logs.
**Frequency:** High  **CEFR level where fixed:** A1

### Error 3: Subject Pronoun Omission
**Linguistic cause:** Arabic verbs carry subject information, so pronouns are often dropped. 
**❌ Example:** Am updating the dashboard.
**✅ Correction:** I am updating the dashboard.
**Frequency:** High  **CEFR level where fixed:** A1

### Error 4: Third Person Singular -s Missing
**Linguistic cause:** Arabic verbs do not require the same person agreement marker as English. 
**❌ Example:** The app work on all devices.
**✅ Correction:** The app works on all devices.
**Frequency:** High  **CEFR level where fixed:** A1

### Error 5: Question Inversion Omission
**Linguistic cause:** Arabic can form questions without auxiliary inversion, unlike English. 
**❌ Example:** You are ready for the demo?
**✅ Correction:** Are you ready for the demo?
**Frequency:** High  **CEFR level where fixed:** A1

### Error 6: Present Continuous Confusion
**Linguistic cause:** Arabic present tense covers ongoing and habitual actions, making English progressive forms hard to distinguish. 
**❌ Example:** I write the code now.
**✅ Correction:** I am writing the code now.
**Frequency:** High  **CEFR level where fixed:** A2

### Error 7: Incorrect Time Preposition for Days
